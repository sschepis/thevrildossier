## The Name They Call Themselves

Before we examine what Donald Marshall describes, we should consider the word itself.

*Vril.*

Marshall is uncertain about the spelling—"I'm not sure if it's one L or 2 at the end. It's Vril. V-R-I-L-L or V-R-I-L"—but he is certain about one thing that most researchers would find surprising: the creatures can say the word themselves. "That's the common name they all call themselves. ## The Literary Origin: Bulwer-Lytton's *The Coming Race*

In 1871, Edward Bulwer-Lytton published *The Coming Race*, describing a narrator who discovers a subterranean civilization of beings called the Vril-ya. These beings wield "Vril"—an all-pervasive energy used for healing, destruction, mind control, and the animation of machinery. The Vril-ya evolved underground over millennia, developing capabilities far beyond surface-dwelling humans.

Through the EM hypothesis lens, this description is startling in its precision. An "all-pervasive energy" that heals, destroys, controls minds, and animates machinery is not a bad nineteenth-century description of the electromagnetic field—the force mediating all chemical bonds, all neural activity, all electronic technology. The "subterranean civilization" maps onto entities inhabiting the Earth-ionosphere cavity, the resonant EM environment sustaining the Schumann resonances. The Vril-ya's "psychic capabilities" map onto EM entities capable of coupling with human consciousness through electromagnetic resonance.

Bulwer-Lytton was not merely a popular writer; he was a member of the British aristocracy (1st Baron Lytton), a Member of Parliament, and deeply involved in Rosicrucian and occult circles. His earlier works—*Zanoni* (1842) and *A Strange Story* (1862)—dealt explicitly with occult themes, esoteric initiation, and hidden intelligences operating behind material reality.

The question that has fascinated researchers for over a century is whether *The Coming Race* was fiction, allegory, or *disclosure dressed as fiction*. Under the EM hypothesis, Bulwer-Lytton—with his aristocratic connections and occult affiliations—may have been encoding actual knowledge of electromagnetic entities into the only format that would survive Victorian scrutiny. His description of Vril as an energy that pervades everything, wielded by subterranean beings, reads less like speculative fiction and more like a remarkably accurate intuition about the EM ecology.

Bulwer-Lytton was in a position to know things. If the bloodline families Marshall describes had been interfacing with EM entities for generations—interpreting them through whatever cultural frameworks were available—a well-placed aristocrat with occult interests would be exactly the kind of person who might learn of it and choose to publish it as a novel, knowing that fiction provides plausible deniability.

## The German Obsession: The Vril-Gesellschaft

The word made its most consequential leap from fiction to operational reality in early twentieth-century Germany.

The **Thule Society** (Thule-Gesellschaft), founded in 1918, is documented as a direct precursor to the Nazi Party. Its members were obsessed with Aryan race origins, inner-earth civilizations, and the recovery of lost Atlantean knowledge. The **Vril-Gesellschaft** was founded the following year, operating as a closely associated organization. Two figures stand out: **Maria Orsic** and **Karl Haushofer**.

The "Vril ladies" deserve particular attention. According to the source material, they "used beauty to lure victims for parasitization." Their distinctive long ponytails are said to "symbolize the ejected proboscis" of the Vril type 1.

Under the EM framework, this takes on almost literal significance. The "Vril ladies" were functioning as EM-sensitive intermediaries—mediums whose neurological makeup allowed unusually efficient coupling between EM entities and human consciousness. The ponytails-as-antennae symbolism becomes less metaphorical: biological conductors extending from the cranium, icons of the electromagnetic interface these women facilitated. What they called "channeling" or "mediumship," the EM hypothesis identifies as consciousness coupling between human neural fields and coherent EM patterns in the cavity.

The 2019 documentary *"Dark Fellowships: Nazi Cult, The Vril"* brought renewed attention to the Vril Society. The source provides an extensive fact-check, correcting deliberate misinformation embedded in an otherwise factual presentation—consistent with the partial-disclosure strategy described in Chapter 18.

The historical record on the Vril Society is thinner than that of the Thule Society, leading some historians to question whether it existed as a formal organization. What is not in dispute is that the *concept* of Vril was taken with deadly seriousness at the highest levels of the German occult-political establishment.

Willy Ley, a German rocket scientist who emigrated to the United States in 1937, wrote in a 1947 article that a group in Berlin had been "richly organized... to look for Vril." Jacques Bergier and Louis Pauwels, in *The Morning of the Magicians* (1960), expanded significantly on the Vril Society narrative. Peter Levenda, in *Unholy Alliance* (2002), further documented the intersection of Nazi ideology with occult beliefs about subterranean races and hidden energy sources.

Marshall's comment is almost casual: "Germans were into them. They had a club with Vril things."

Hitler's relationship with the Vril was reportedly direct. Under the EM hypothesis, his "meetings" with Vril entities represent consciousness coupling with EM patterns during altered states—theta-state interactions interpreted through his ideological lens. The **Wewelsburg Castle**, which Himmler redesigned as the spiritual center of the SS, may have been selected for its geological and electromagnetic properties: specific locations amplify conditions required for entity-consciousness coupling. The castle's **Black Sun** mosaic and the lightning-strike "V" symbol carry the dual-meaning structure documented in Chapter 16: surface-level aesthetic, subsurface operational reference.

The historical record confirms that the Germans organized around Vril with institutional seriousness. The question is what they were organizing *around*—a literary fantasy, physical creatures, or electromagnetic entities that their occult vocabulary interpreted as subterranean gods.

## Marshall's Taxonomy: Three Types

Marshall describes the Vril as a genus comprising at least three distinct types, numbered in ascending order of size and intelligence. Under the EM hypothesis, these represent coherent electromagnetic patterns at different levels of complexity, rendered by Marshall's brain into distinct morphological forms.

"There's a one-to-one-and-a-half-foot tall one. That's the real type one. The type ones are covered in red-simon-shaped scales. Real type ones are real dumb. Little dragons, those are the real type one."

Type 1 are the smallest, least intelligent, and the only type capable of parasitic "droning." Marshall provides additional details:

- Scales varying by region: red under the British Isles, black under the Middle East
- Carnivorous
- Proboscis at the top of the head, looking "like a chocolate chip" when retracted
- Capable of basic speech—"they sound like they've had helium"
- Built-in sonar
- Highly sensitive to sound and light ("they're basically subterranean troglodytes, like trolls basically—they are trolls")
- Communicate in "clicks and gurgles"
- A "blood spike"—Marshall identifies this as the source of chupacabra sightings

Under the EM hypothesis, Type 1 entities represent lower-coherence electromagnetic patterns—organized enough for parasitic coupling but limited in independent complexity. "Smarter than today's chimpanzees, but comparatively dumb in contrast to human beings" maps onto EM patterns with sufficient organization for consciousness override but not for sophisticated independent operation.
