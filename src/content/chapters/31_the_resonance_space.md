## The Problem with the Physical Reading

Marshall's account of the "cloning center" presents a vivid, internally consistent picture: an underground facility, typically Canadian, with a gymnasium-like arena surrounded by stadium seating. World leaders, celebrities, and military figures attend. Decisions are made. Entertainment is performed. Punishments are administered. The same facility, visited consistently during REM sleep over years.

The physical reading of this account — that literal underground facilities exist with literal clone bodies in literal tanks — generates a set of logistical problems that Chapter 29 addressed in part:

1. **Construction scale**: Building and maintaining facilities capable of housing hundreds of clone bodies, hosting gatherings of world leaders, and operating medical/technological infrastructure — in secret, across multiple locations — would require construction on a scale comparable to NORAD's Cheyenne Mountain Complex, replicated multiple times.

2. **Clone maintenance**: Biological bodies require nutrition, waste removal, temperature regulation, and medical monitoring. Maintaining hundreds of dormant clone bodies in ready-to-activate status would require hospital-grade infrastructure running continuously.

3. **Transportation logistics**: If consciousness is transferred to physical clones during REM sleep, the consciousness must traverse the distance between the sleeper and the clone body. Marshall has described being in Canada while sleeping in his bed. How does the consciousness travel thousands of miles to a specific underground location?

4. **Detection**: Any physical facility of this scale would produce detectable signatures: heat, electromagnetic emissions, personnel traffic, supply chains, waste products. None has been detected.

These objections are not decisive — classified facilities exist that have evaded detection for decades. But they accumulate. Each one adds friction. Together, they make the physical reading *possible* but *strained*.

This chapter proposes an alternative reading that resolves all four problems simultaneously.

## Consciousness as Electromagnetic Field

The proposition that consciousness is an electromagnetic phenomenon is not New Age speculation. It is a peer-reviewed scientific hypothesis with growing empirical support.

### The CEMI Theory (Johnjoe McFadden, 2002-2020)

The Conscious Electromagnetic Information (CEMI) theory, developed by molecular biologist Johnjoe McFadden at the University of Surrey, proposes that consciousness arises from the brain's electromagnetic field — not from the neurons themselves, but from the field generated by their collective activity.

Key arguments:

- The brain generates a measurable electromagnetic field through the synchronized firing of billions of neurons
- This field is not an epiphenomenon — it feeds back onto neural activity, influencing which neurons fire (a process called "field-coupling")
- The field provides the **binding** that neuroscience has struggled to explain: how do disparate neural processes (visual, auditory, emotional, motor) combine into a unified conscious experience? The EM field integrates all neural activity simultaneously
- The field persists even when individual neurons die or are replaced — explaining why consciousness is continuous despite cellular turnover

McFadden's 2020 paper in *Neuroscience of Consciousness* presents extensive evidence for the theory and argues that the EM field is not merely correlated with consciousness — it *is* consciousness.

### Orch-OR (Penrose and Hameroff, 1994-2024)

The Orchestrated Objective Reduction theory, developed by mathematician Roger Penrose and anesthesiologist Stuart Hameroff, proposes that consciousness arises from quantum processes in microtubules within neurons. While different from CEMI in mechanism, Orch-OR shares a crucial feature: it treats consciousness as operating at a level *below* the neuron — at the quantum level, where electromagnetic interactions are fundamental.

Both theories point in the same direction: consciousness is not software running on neural hardware. It is an electromagnetic or quantum phenomenon that uses neural hardware as its substrate but is not reducible to it.

A standing wave is an electromagnetic pattern that remains stable in a fixed location. Unlike a traveling wave (which propagates outward), a standing wave maintains its shape while oscillating in place. It is created by the constructive and destructive interference of waves reflecting between boundaries.

If consciousness-patterns can be sustained by organized electromagnetic standing waves, then the Schumann resonances provide a **persistent, spatially structured, globally accessible** substrate for such patterns. A consciousness-pattern sustained by a Schumann standing wave would:
